Context

From franchise-first retail to digital commerce capabilities.

Alain Afflelou is one of France's historic optics brands with an international footprint - but a business model built on franchises, not digital commerce. The digital team needed to accelerate on several fronts simultaneously: launch e-commerce for optics, add a contact lens subscription, and replatform Malentille.com.

The challenge: multichannel experience across several business lines, multiple stakeholders with different agendas, and no existing design culture, tooling, or process to build on.

The real challenge. Management strategy and priorities shifted repeatedly throughout the mission. Staying productive and maintaining design quality while adapting to frequent pivots - in an organization with no established design culture to anchor decisions - was the defining constraint of this project.

Mission trajectory

Phase 1 - Embedded via UX Republic

Build momentum from the inside: audit, guerilla tests, co-design rituals, early UI Kit work, e-commerce flows and team upskilling.

Phase 2 - Direct mission

Finalize the design system and UI handoffs, pass the practice to internal teams and external providers, and coach middle management.

Method and approach

A qualitative, iterative design practice embedded in delivery.

A quantitative study was considered and set aside: too early, and budget was tight. Instead, the mission relied on continuous guerilla testing, co-design and delivery-cycle learning.

01

Audit and scoping

UI/UX audit, collaborative workspace setup, stakeholder mapping and needs framing with digital, marketing, process and field actors.

02

User research

Guerilla tests: preparation, facilitation, synthesis. Continuous loop: test, fail, learn, iterate.

03

Co-design and production

Workshops, wireframes, high-fidelity mockups, prototypes, feasibility checks, roadmap input and UX writing.

04

Design Ops and team building

Migration to Figma, UI Kit, design system, recruitment, upskilling and coaching toward digital UI.

05

Handoff and acculturation

Design system finalization, UI handoffs, passations, UX coaching and evangelization with product, dev and marketing.

Tools: Figma - Miro - Testapic - Trello - Git - Google Suite - Teams

Impact

The strongest signal: the client came back directly.

A fully operational design team at the end of the mission - from zero to structured practice with tooling, process and people in place.

The design system became the shared reference for all internal and external contributors. Most telling signal: Afflelou re-engaged directly after the non-compete clause to finalize the design system and UI handoffs - the foundation held without the original designer.

Learning

The first month buys the next six.

Starting with UI cleanup and quick-and-dirty fixes felt like a step down - work a junior could handle. It took time to see it for what it was: the only way in.

In an organization with no design culture, you earn strategic scope by delivering on the mundane first. The design studio did not happen despite the cleanup work - it happened because of it. I now factor this in explicitly when scoping embedded missions.
